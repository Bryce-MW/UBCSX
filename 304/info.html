New User

The new user page is shown to you when you first log in, before you have an entry in our database. Here you give us your name since we do not know that from just your CWL, and you give us a nickname for your first account. If you navigate to this page again after you already are in our database, filling out the form will change your name and create an additional account. We recommend using the add account page to add an additional account after you already have one. You may create as many accounts as you like.

Accounts

The main page shows you an overview of your account and all of your positions. The account which has its info showing will have a purple outline. If you do not see a purple outline, then you have not selected an account somehow or we broke something. In that case, you will probably see lots of zeros. Please click on an account name to switch to it. The accounts box also has a plus button which you can click to do the the add account page.

Cash is the amount of cash that you have available to buy stocks and market value is the current value of your shares and ETFs. As of writing this, the value of your options and loaned shares are not included and your lent shares are not deducted. This requires writing some more SQL but may be done in the future. The total equity is the sum of your cash and market value. Open orders is the number of orders that you have placed which are not fulfilled and daily maintenance is the amount which you must pay daily to maintain the shares which you have loaned. This does not have deducted the income that you receive from lending shares yet but this may be improved with some more SQL in the future. Since all accounts start with the same amount of money, your closed profit is how much money you have now divided by how much you started with and the total profit is your total equity divided by how much money you started with.

The table contains all of your current positions. As of writing this, it does not yet include ETFs and options but that is planned if we have enough time. The last price is the last price that a share was traded at and is only available for shares, not options or ETFs. ETFs will use the present value of one share instead. This value is used to calculate the value of your positions. For options, the value uses the bid-ask spread. The bid is the highest price a buyer is willing to pay and the ask is the lowest price a seller is willing to receive. The market value is the value of the position as mentioned multiplied by the number of that position that you own. The percent of portfolio is the value of this position divided by your market value. The count is how many of this position you have. Click on one of the symbols in the symbol column to go to the page for that symbol.

Info

This is the page that you are on now! This page describes all of the other pages, their functionality, and any caveats due to something not being implemented yet.

Settings

The settings page allows you to change your name. Currently your name only effects the name listed as the CEO of any company which you IPO. Changing your name will not change the CEO of any existing company. In the future, this page will also allow you to delete an account or your entire user. If you delete an account (or your user which will delete all of your accounts), all positions that you owned will disappear, lent shares will go back to their owner and loaned shares will be revoked. If you delete your user, your name will still stay associated with all companies that you are the CEO of.

Search

This page shows you the search results for whatever you typed in the search bar. It looks at both symbols and company names. It will shows both stocks and ETFs. It will also give you the price for them. Click on a symbol or name to go to the symbols page for that symbol. There is a create button which will lead you to the IPO page with the symbol or name field pre-filled with your query depending on which we guess that it is.

Add Account

You can use this page to create a new account by giving it a name. One of the simplest pages on the site.

Orders

The orders page shows you all of your orders in a table and allows you to delete them. You may also place new orders. Make sure not to select an option order if you are ordering an ETF and don't enter an expiry or strike price if you are not making an option order. It won't do anything if you enter them but it might confuse you. Unfortunately, it is difficult to remove those fields when they are not applicable without using Javascript which is something that we tried to avoid in this project.

Symbols

This page shows you all of the information about a symbol or option that you choose. The last price is the last price that a share was traded at. The outstanding shares is the total number of shares for that company that currently exist. Lent percent is the number of shares of that company which have been lent divided by the number of outstanding shares. The lending available shows the total number of shares of any premium which are available for lending. The open orders is the number of orders (shown in the table below) which exist for the stock, option, or ETF. Some of these fields may not be present for options or ETFs or may have a slightly different meaning. The place new order and lend / loan buttons take to to the orders page and the lending / loaning pages respectively. The open orders table shows you all current orders for the stock or option. It is not shown when an ETF is selected.

IPO

The IPO page is used to take a new company public. If creating an ETF, the company name must be the name of one of your accounts. If creating an ETF, do not enter a number of shares. It will not do anything if you do but it may confuse you. Note that you will not be able to create more shares later. You will also have a chance to update your CEO quote. Note that this quote is tied to you as a CEO for all companies that you are the CEO of but it will not effect any companies that you were the CEO of with a previous name.

Lending / Loaning

Lending and Loaning can be done on this page by offering or taking an existing offer to lend. When offering, you specify the APR premium that you are willing to allow and how many shares you will offer at that premium. When taking an offer, you specify the maximum premium that you are willing to accept. You will take all shares below that premium until you have taken the number that you requested or no more shares are available below that premium.
